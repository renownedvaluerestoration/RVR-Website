Soft Washing vs Pressure Washing: What Your Home Actually Needs

Many homeowners don't realize that pressure washing can actually damage certain surfaces. Soft washing uses specialized low-pressure nozzles and eco-friendly detergents to clean without risking damage to delicate materials.

For vinyl siding, stucco, and older brickwork in historic Bloomfield homes, soft washing is essential. It removes mold, algae, and dirt at the root level without blasting away protective surfaces or forcing water behind walls.

Safety Procedures & Operational Standards

Your safety and property protection are our top priorities. We follow strict protocols on every job.

CERTIFIED SAFETY

Committed to Your Safety

We strictly enforce comprehensive safety procedures to protect our team, your property, and the environment.

Personal Protective Equipment (PPE)

ANSI-Approved Eye Protection

Our technician always wears ANSI-approved safety goggles or full-face shields to prevent injury from 4200 PSI pressure or debris.

Thermal Protection

We wear insulated, waterproof gloves and steel-toed boots to protect our team from high-velocity debris and scalding water reaching temperatures up to 210°F.

Two-Handed Operation

Our team maintains a firm, two-handled grip on the trigger gun at all times to safely manage the "kickback" force of our 4.0 GPM flow.

Hazard Prevention & Environmental Safety

Carbon Monoxide Mitigation

We operate our gas engines and diesel burners exclusively in well-ventilated outdoor areas and never run equipment inside enclosed spaces like garages or vans.

Fire Safety Zones

We keep a minimum 15-foot clearance between our diesel burner's exhaust stack and any flammable materials, including your home's siding and nearby landscaping.

Grey Water Management

We direct & collect grey water with diversion from storm drains & retrieval into greywater tank when using potent chemicals.

Biodegradable Solutions

We use Biodegradable Degreasers in large areas: Our use of EBC ensures that heavy oils are broken down into environmentally safe byproducts.

Equipment & Chemical Safety

Daily Equipment Inspections

We check our high-pressure hoses for kinks, bubbles, or wear every morning and replace any compromised equipment immediately to ensure a safe job site.

Complete SDS Documentation

We maintain a complete SDS (Safety Data Sheet) binder on every job site for all chemicals, including SH, EBC, and Ure-Seal, as required by Michigan Right-to-Know laws.

Pressure Relief Protocols

After every shut-down, we bleed the trapped pressure from the lines before disconnecting, ensuring no accidental high-pressure releases occur.

Cool Down Cycle

We follow a strict "cool down" cycle: We turn off our diesel burners several minutes before the job ends, running cold water through the system to protect our equipment and ensure no scalding water remains in the lines.

Strict Dilution Ratios

We strictly follow dilution ratios: We never use "raw" chemicals; our downstream injection systems ensure that only the minimum effective amount of product is used on your home.

Property & Site Safety

Saturation Protection

Before and after chemical application, we flood all surrounding vegetation with fresh water to ensure your landscaping remains healthy.

Secure Work Zones

We secure the work zone: We use safety cones and "Caution" tape to clearly mark our work area, protecting residents and pets from slip hazards and hot water runoff.

Stable Equipment Placement

We ensure stable equipment placement: We operate our machinery on level ground to prevent fuel spills and ensure the internal components of our Simpson ALH 4240 function safely and efficiently.

House Wash Procedures

Vinyl Siding Soft Wash

1

Saturate all surrounding landscaping with fresh water to create a protective barrier

2

Clear siding of loose debris, spider webs, and nests using low-pressure rinse

3

Apply Sodium Hypochlorite and surfactant solution from bottom to top

4

Allow 10-15 minute dwell time, then rinse thoroughly from top down

Brick or Stucco Soft Wash

1

Extended saturation of porous masonry to prevent chemical absorption

2

Apply stronger chemical blend with increased surfactant ratio

3

15-20 minute dwell time for deep pore penetration

4

Ultra-low pressure rinse (below 100 PSI) to protect masonry
